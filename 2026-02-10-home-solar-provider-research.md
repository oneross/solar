## Part 2: New Jersey Specific Considerations (2026)

### 2.1 Current Incentives

| Incentive | Value | Duration | Notes |
|-----------|-------|----------|-------|
| **SREC-II (SuSI Program)** | $85/MWh | 15 years | Fixed rate, ~$7,000-$9,000 lifetime value |
| **Federal ITC** | 30% | N/A | ‚ùå **No longer available for purchases in 2026** |
| **Property Tax Exemption** | 100% | Permanent | Solar adds no property tax |
| **Sales Tax Exemption** | 6.625% | Permanent | No sales tax on system purchase |
| **Net Metering** | 1:1 retail rate | Until program changes | Full credit for excess energy |

**Critical 2026 Change:** The direct federal tax credit is gone. The only way for homeowners to access ITC benefits now is through third-party ownership (leases/PPAs), where the financing company claims the credit and passes savings to you.

### 2.2 Net Metering Details

- **PSE&G** (serves Ramsey/Hawthorne area): 1:1 net metering
- Annual true-up: Credits roll over monthly, settled annually
- Excess credits at end of year paid at wholesale rate (lower value)
- Proper system sizing is critical: match production to consumption

### 2.3 Utility Considerations

| Utility | Coverage | Net Metering | Grid Access Fee |
|---------|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| PSE&G | Bergen County | Yes, 1:1 | ~$8-12/month remains |
| JCP&L | Parts of NJ | Yes, 1:1 | Similar |

**Note:** You'll always pay a small grid access fee (~$8/month) even with solar. This is normal and expected.

### 2.4 Local Permitting (Bergen County)

- **Building permit required** for all solar installations
- **Electrical permit** required
- **Utility interconnection agreement** with PSE&G
- Permit timeline: 2-6 weeks typical
- Good installers handle all permitting as part of their service

## Part 4: Old House Considerations

If Hawthorne or Ramsey home is older (pre-1970):

### Structural Assessment Requirements

| Factor | Threshold | Action Required |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Panel Weight** | 3-4 lbs/sq ft | Most roofs handle 20+ lbs/sq ft |
| **Total System Weight** | 600-2,400 lbs typical | Calculate for your array size |
| **Roof Age** | >10-15 years | Consider replacement before solar |
| **Pre-1970 Home** | Automatic | Structural engineer assessment |
| **Visible Sagging** | Any | Structural engineer required |
| **Rafter/Truss Condition** | Must inspect | Check for rot, decay |

### Cost Implications

| Scenario | Additional Cost | Recommendation |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Roof replacement needed | $8,000-$15,000 | Do before solar installation |
| Structural reinforcement | $2,000-$15,000 | Get engineering assessment |
| Ground mount alternative | $5,000-$15,000 premium | Consider if roof unsuitable |

**Key Rule:** If your roof needs replacement in the next 5-7 years, replace it BEFORE installing solar. Removing and reinstalling panels for a roof replacement costs $3,000-$8,000.

## Part 5: Home Sale Implications

This is **critically important** given the 2026 regulatory environment.

### Owned vs. Leased When Selling

| Factor | Owned Solar | Leased/PPA Solar |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| **Home Value Impact** | +3-10% ($9,000-$15,000+) | No increase, possible hindrance |
| **Sale Complexity** | Simple transfer | Buyer must qualify for lease |
| **Buyer Perception** | Asset | Liability |
| **Deal Risk** | Low | High (can kill deals) |
| **Transfer Process** | Include in sale | Requires lender approval, fees |

### The 2026 Dilemma

Since the federal tax credit is only available through PPAs in 2026:

**If you plan to sell within 5-7 years:**
- Consider whether the tax credit savings outweigh sale complications
- Negotiate strong buyout terms (prorated, clear pricing)
- Get the buyout schedule in writing before signing

**If you plan to stay long-term (10+ years):**
- PPA may make sense for the credit capture
- 25-year contract is less concerning if you're staying

3. What happens if you sell before year 5?
4. Can the buyer refuse the lease transfer?

## Part 7: Recommended Questions for Provider Meetings

### Financial Questions
1. What is the total cost over 25 years including all escalators?
2. What are my exact monthly payments for years 1, 5, 10, 15, 20, 25?
3. What is the complete buyout schedule (every year)?
4. What happens if I sell my home before the contract ends?
5. What financing partners do you use? (Check their stability)

### Technical Questions
1. What panel brand and model will be installed?
2. String inverter or microinverters? Why?
3. What is the guaranteed production (kWh/year)?
4. Who does the actual installation? (In-house or subcontractors?)
5. What happens if production is below guarantee?

### Service Questions
1. What's your average permit-to-power timeline?
2. What is your service response time for issues?
3. Who do I call if there's a problem in 10 years?
4. What happens to my warranty if your company is sold or closes?
5. Can I speak to 3 customers in my area?

### Company Stability Questions
1. How long have you been in business?
2. What is your annual installation volume?
3. Have you had any financial difficulties or ownership changes?
4. Are you the installing company or a sales company?
5. Can you provide bank or financial references?
